# Impact-of-Smoking-on-Human-Weight-Using-R
Smokers often experience weight gain shortly after quitting, and this temporary effect can reduce some of the immediate health benefits associated with smoking cessation. This study reviews relevant literature to examine how smoking habits, dietary choices, and physical activity collectively influence weight in the human body.

The results of the multiple regression analysis indicate that factors other than nicotine—which is known to suppress appetite—play a significant role in predicting weight. These additional predictors may include genetic influences and other behavioral or environmental factors. Furthermore, the findings from the logistic regression model suggest that the likelihood of gaining or losing weight is not an effective predictor for preventing obesity.
